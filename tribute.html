Agatha Mary Clarissa Christie

The Queen of crimes
"Agatha Christie was an English writer. She is known for her 66 detective novels and 14 short story collections, particularly those revolving around her fictional detectives Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple."

    Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1890 - Christie was born into a wealthy upper-middle-class family in Torquay, Devon.
  • 1920 -She was initially an unsuccessful writer with six consecutive rejections, but this changed when The Mysterious Affair at Styles, featuring Hercule Poirot, was published in 1920
  • 1919 - Christie, meanwhile, settled into married life, giving birth to her only child, Rosalind Margaret Hicks, at Ashfield, where the couple spent much of their time, having few friends in London.
  • 1926 - Archie asked Agatha for a divorce.
  • 1930 - Christie left England for Istanbul and subsequently for Baghdad on the Orient Express. Late in this trip, in 1930, she met a young archaeologist 13 years her junior, Max Mallowan, whom she married.
  • 1934 - Christie's novel Murder on the Orient Express was written in the Pera Palace Hotel in Istanbul, Turkey, the southern terminus of the railway. The hotel maintains Christie's room as a memorial to the author..
  • 1942 - The British intelligence agency MI5 investigated Christie after a character called Major Bletchley appeared in her 1941 thriller N or M?, which was about a hunt for a pair of deadly fifth columnists in wartime England. MI5 was afraid that Christie had a spy in Britain's top-secret codebreaking centre, Bletchley Park.
  • 1976 - Dame Agatha Christie died at age 85 from natural causes at her home Winterbrook House which was located in Winterbrook, Wallingford, Oxfordshire.
